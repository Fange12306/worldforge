The alarm tore through the Dawnbreaker's quiet.

"Three destroyers, distance zero-point-two light-years, dead ahead!" The tactical officer's voice nearly cracked. "Six more frigates flanking—"

Lin Yifeng was already fully awake from his rest cycle. Ion storm data surged across the holographic display — right on schedule. The Abyss Nebula erupted with massive ion turbulence every thirty-six standard hours, and they had hit this window head-on.

Shadow Grip had chosen this exact moment to attack, meaning they were either arrogant beyond measure or their intelligence was disturbingly precise.

"Kill main engines. Divert all energy to forward shield arc."

"Captain — we'll lose all mobility!"

"In an ion storm, mobility is an illusion." Lin Yifeng's fingers slid across the holographic display. "Starboard thirty degrees. In three seconds, maximum engine thrust — reverse."

No one questioned the second order. The Dawnbreaker's massive hull flipped like a leaf caught in ion turbulence, Shadow Grip's first wave of torpedoes skimming past the belly of the ship.

"All hands, brace for impact!"

The ion storm's front slammed into the Dawnbreaker. Shields flickered violently. But the storm also enveloped Shadow Grip's fleet — three destroyers were forced to scatter formation, and two frigates' shields had already overloaded, their hulls exposed to the ion torrent.

This was not a battle of strength against strength. It was experience against numbers.

"The nebula holds more than storms." Lin Yifeng's voice cut through the clamor of the bridge with unnatural clarity. "Activate gravitational sensors — find the largest vortex."

The holographic display surfaced a dark red spiral pattern — a gravitational singularity vortex stirred up by the ion storm, less than half a light-year across, but with a gravitational gradient capable of tearing apart anything that drew too close.

"Draw them in." Lin Yifeng said.

The Dawnbreaker drifted like a wounded whale, slow and ponderous, toward the gravitational vortex. Shadow Grip's fleet pursued relentlessly — three destroyers had already closed the distance, and cannon fire began striking the Dawnbreaker's shields.

"Closer..."

"Captain, shields at seventeen percent!"

"All hands — hard turn starboard, forty-five degrees!"

The Dawnbreaker suddenly burst with speed it shouldn't have had, its hull tracing a sharp arc along the edge of the gravitational vortex. Shadow Grip's pursuit formation couldn't adjust in time — the two leading destroyers were caught by the edge of the vortex, their hulls beginning to be torn apart by tidal forces.

The comms channel filled with the shriek of twisting metal and the desperate cries of doomed crew.

Zak Bloodfist watched it all from the bridge of the Bloodfist, his mechanical left arm's joints emitting faint grinding sounds.

"Retreat." He said.

"Leader—"

"I said retreat." His voice ground like crushed gravel. "The gravity vortex won't kill him. Something else in this nebula will. That man — he'll come back on his own."

Shadow Grip's remaining fleet vanished into the violet electrical glow of the ion storm. On the bridge of the Dawnbreaker, Lin Yifeng wore no expression of victory. He knew Zak Bloodfist was right — they were trapped in the Abyss Nebula, and the outside world held both a Federation bounty and a Shadow Grip price on their heads.
